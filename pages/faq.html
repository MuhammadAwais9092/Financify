Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common mortgage and lending questions

How much house can I afford?

Generally, you can afford a home that costs 2.5 to 3 times your annual gross income. However, this depends on several factors including your down payment, credit score, monthly debts, and current interest rates.

A good rule of thumb is that your total monthly housing costs (including principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and HOA fees) should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income.

Use our mortgage calculator to get a more personalized estimate based on your specific financial situation.

What credit score do I need to buy a home?

Credit score requirements vary by loan type:

  • Conventional loans: Typically 620+ (best rates at 740+)
  • FHA loans: As low as 580 with 3.5% down, or 500 with 10% down
  • VA loans: No official minimum, but most lenders prefer 620+
  • USDA loans: Typically 640+

Higher credit scores generally qualify for better interest rates and loan terms.

How much do I need for a down payment?

Down payment requirements vary by loan program:

  • Conventional loans: As low as 3% for first-time buyers
  • FHA loans: 3.5% with credit score 580+
  • VA loans: 0% down for eligible veterans
  • USDA loans: 0% down in eligible rural areas

While you can put down less than 20%, you may need to pay private mortgage insurance (PMI) until you reach 20% equity.

What documents do I need to apply for a mortgage?

You'll typically need the following documents:

  • Pay stubs from the last 30 days
  • Tax returns from the last 2 years
  • W-2s from the last 2 years
  • Bank statements from the last 2-3 months
  • Investment account statements
  • Photo identification
  • Proof of any additional income
  • Documentation of gifts or down payment assistance

Self-employed borrowers may need additional documentation such as profit and loss statements and business bank statements.

How long does the mortgage application process take?

The typical timeline is:

  • Pre-approval: 1-3 business days
  • Application to closing: 30-45 days
  • Underwriting: 7-14 days
  • Appraisal: 7-10 days

At Financify, we streamline the process with digital tools and can often close loans in as little as 15 days for qualified borrowers.

What's the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?

Pre-qualification is an initial assessment based on information you provide about your income, assets, and debts. It gives you a rough estimate of what you might be able to borrow.

Pre-approval is a more thorough process where we verify your financial information and check your credit. You'll receive a pre-approval letter that shows sellers you're a serious buyer with confirmed financing.

Pre-approval is stronger and more reliable for making offers on homes.

What factors affect my mortgage interest rate?

Several factors influence your mortgage rate:

  • Credit score: Higher scores get better rates
  • Down payment: Larger down payments reduce risk
  • Loan term: Shorter terms typically have lower rates
  • Loan type: Conventional, FHA, VA loans have different rate structures
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Lower ratios are favorable
  • Property type: Single-family homes usually get the best rates
  • Market conditions: Economic factors affect all rates

Should I choose a fixed-rate or adjustable-rate mortgage?

Fixed-rate mortgages have the same interest rate for the entire loan term, providing predictable monthly payments. They're ideal if you plan to stay in your home long-term or prefer payment stability.

Adjustable-rate mortgages (ARMs) start with a lower rate for an initial period, then adjust periodically. They can save money if you plan to move or refinance within the initial fixed period.

Consider your financial situation, how long you plan to stay in the home, and your risk tolerance when choosing.

When should I lock my interest rate?

Rate locks protect you from interest rate increases during your loan process. Consider locking when:

  • You're satisfied with the current rate
  • You have a signed purchase agreement
  • Rates are expected to rise
  • You're within 30-60 days of closing

Rate locks typically last 30-60 days, with extensions available if needed. We'll help you determine the best timing for your situation.

What's the difference between FHA and conventional loans?

FHA loans are government-backed and offer:

  • Lower down payment (3.5%)
  • More flexible credit requirements
  • Higher debt-to-income ratios allowed
  • Mortgage insurance required

Conventional loans are not government-backed and offer:

  • Higher loan limits
  • No upfront mortgage insurance
  • PMI can be removed at 20% equity
  • Better rates for high-credit borrowers

Am I eligible for a VA loan?

You may be eligible for a VA loan if you're:

  • An active-duty service member
  • A veteran who served for a minimum period
  • A surviving spouse of a service member who died in service or from a service-connected disability
  • A member of the National Guard or Reserves who served for at least 6 years

VA loans offer 0% down payment, no PMI, and competitive interest rates. You'll need a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) from the VA.

What is a jumbo loan and do I need one?

A jumbo loan exceeds the conforming loan limits set by the Federal Housing Finance Agency. For 2024, the limit is $766,550 in most areas (higher in expensive markets).

You'll need a jumbo loan if your loan amount exceeds these limits. Jumbo loans typically require:

  • Higher credit scores (700+)
  • Larger down payments (10-20%)
  • Lower debt-to-income ratios
  • Larger cash reserves

Despite stricter requirements, jumbo loan rates are often competitive with conventional loans.

What are closing costs and how much should I expect to pay?

Closing costs are fees associated with finalizing your mortgage and typically include:

  • Loan origination fees
  • Appraisal and inspection fees
  • Title insurance and escrow fees
  • Recording fees and taxes
  • Prepaid insurance and property taxes

Total closing costs typically range from 2-5% of the loan amount. For a $400,000 loan, expect $8,000-$20,000 in closing costs.

You can sometimes negotiate with the seller to pay some of your closing costs or choose a higher interest rate to cover them.

What happens at closing?

At closing, you'll:

  • Review and sign loan documents
  • Review the Closing Disclosure
  • Provide a certified check for closing costs and down payment
  • Do a final walkthrough of the property
  • Get the keys to your new home!

The closing typically takes 1-2 hours and involves you, the seller, real estate agents, and a closing agent or attorney.

Can I make changes to my loan after closing?

After closing, your loan terms are set, but you have several options to modify your mortgage:

  • Refinancing: Get a new loan with different terms or rate
  • Loan modification: Work with your lender to modify existing terms (typically for hardship situations)
  • Recasting: Make a large principal payment to reduce monthly payments
  • Extra payments: Pay additional principal to pay off the loan faster

We can help you explore refinancing options when it makes financial sense.

What is private mortgage insurance (PMI)?

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) protects the lender if you default on your loan. It's typically required when you put down less than 20% on a conventional loan.

PMI costs typically range from 0.3% to 1.5% of the original loan amount annually, paid monthly. Once you reach 20% equity in your home, you can usually request PMI removal.

PMI automatically terminates when you reach 22% equity based on the original amortization schedule.

Should I pay points to lower my interest rate?

Discount points allow you to "buy down" your interest rate by paying additional upfront costs. Each point typically costs 1% of your loan amount and reduces your rate by about 0.25%.

Points make sense if:

  • You plan to stay in the home long enough to break even
  • You have extra cash and want to reduce monthly payments
  • You're in a high tax bracket (points may be deductible)

We'll help you calculate the break-even point to determine if points are worthwhile for your situation.
